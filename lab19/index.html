2000s

Clothing from the 2000s was directly influenced by globalization, the rise of fast fashion, and celebrities as style icons.


Technology and Y2K had a huge impact on fashion in the early 2000s. The color palette was filled with shiny black tones and reflective metallics. Denim became a staple for men and women, going beyond jeans to shirts, jackets, and hats.


As the decade went on, 2000s fashion began to take cues from 1960s bohemian looks. Low-rise jeans, cowl-neck shirts, capri pants, cropped jackets, and dresses over jeans were popular outfit choices for women. For men outfit options included light wash bootcut jeans, cargo shorts, classic rock t-shirts, fitted cowboy shirts, and polos with popped collars.


Towards the late 2000s neon colors, animal prints, geometric shapes, and light denim jeggings were reintroduced from previous decades.
